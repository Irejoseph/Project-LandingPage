What we offer

Essential Digital Skills Bootcamps

These Bootcamps are designed to equip individuals with the practical, everyday tech skills needed to succeed in both personal and professional settings. Over the course of 4 to 6 weeks, participants will gain hands-on experience with tools like Microsoft Office, calendar management, and file organization. These bootcamps aim to boost confidence, improve digital fluency, and open doors to new opportunities in today’s technology-driven world.

For Volunteers

Who can volunteer as a tutor? If you’re confident with digital tools and enjoy helping others learn, you’re welcome — even if you’ve never taught before! We’ll provide guidance and materials to support you.
How much time do I need to commit? You can start with as little as 1 hour per week. Most tutors teach 1–2 hours weekly depending on their availability.
Do I get any rewards or recognition? Yes! You’ll earn points for every session you teach, which can be redeemed for gift cards and certificates. You’ll also get shoutouts and be eligible for “Tutor of the Month.”
How do I apply to be a tutor? Click I Want to Volunteer on the homepage and complete a short registration form. We'll be in touch with next steps!
Can I volunteer remotely? Yes — most of our tutors work with learners online from anywhere in the UK. All you need is internet access, a device, and time.
Do I need a DBS check to volunteer? Not initially, but if you plan to work with vulnerable adults or partner organizations, we may request one later. We’ll guide you through it if needed.
